About me

I'm from Huntingburg, Indiana, and currently study Computer Science at Purdue University. After I graduate, my plan is to work as a Software Developer.

Although I'm a programmer and tech enthusiast, I might be a liberal arts student at heart. In addition to major in CS, I'm also pursuing a degree in Spanish, and have made contributions to the Purdue Exponent, one of the nation's top student-run collegiate newspapers.

Outside of school, I enjoy practicing my Spanish, cooking, traveling, reading (mostly non-fiction), and the occasional bike ride. In 2025, I plan to go on a semester-long study abroad to either Mexico or Colombia, where I will continue my studies and hone my working proficiency of Spanish.

I am very excited about someday working in the Software Development industry. We are currently amidst a great technological revolution—technologies like generative AI, virtual reality, and quantum computing are only in their infancy. It will be a privilege to work with and develop these technologies in my career, and I hope to make my own small impact on the future.

One of my core philosophies is that good software and products are dependent upon thoughtful, user-oriented engineers. Even the smallest decisions will inevitably affect the end user.

During an Apple event, Steve Jobs once said, "Technology alone is not enough—that's technology, married with liberal arts... that yields us the result that make our hearts sing."

This was Apple's core philosophy, one which I identify with greatly. It's the reason why Apple, and not IBM, HP, or Microsoft, is the world's most successful company. Steve Jobs did not just have an engineering perspective, but also a humanistic one.

In my career, I hope I can leverage my unique background to create software that embodies this philosophy.
